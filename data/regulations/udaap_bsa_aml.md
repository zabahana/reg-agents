# UDAAP and BSA/AML

## UDAAP — Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts or Practices
An act or practice is unfair if it causes or is likely to cause substantial
injury to consumers that is not reasonably avoidable and not outweighed by
benefits. Fraud controls that block legitimate customers without recourse, or
opaque decisions that consumers cannot understand or contest, can raise UDAAP
concerns. Provide clear reasons and remediation paths.

## BSA/AML — Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering
Banks must maintain risk-based programs to detect and report suspicious
activity (SARs) and comply with customer due diligence (CDD) and know-your-
customer (KYC) requirements. Transaction-monitoring and fraud models used for
AML should be validated, explainable, and tuned to balance detection against
false positives.

## Model Explainability for Consumer-Facing Decisions
Where an automated model contributes to blocking transactions or accounts,
institutions should be able to document the drivers of the decision, support
human review of high-impact actions, and monitor for bias across customer
segments.

## Real-Time Decisioning Controls
Low-latency fraud decisioning must include fallback logic, override paths for
analysts, and logging sufficient for audit and dispute resolution.
