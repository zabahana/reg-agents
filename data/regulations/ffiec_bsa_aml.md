# FFIEC BSA/AML Examination Manual

## Program Pillars
A BSA/AML compliance program must include, at minimum: (1) a system of internal
controls; (2) independent testing; (3) a designated BSA compliance officer;
(4) training; and (5) risk-based customer due diligence (the "fifth pillar").

## Risk-Based Approach
Banks should identify and assess money-laundering and terrorist-financing risks
across products, services, customers, and geographies, and calibrate controls
accordingly. The risk assessment informs transaction-monitoring thresholds.

## Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and KYC
CDD requires understanding the nature and purpose of customer relationships to
develop a customer risk profile, and conducting ongoing monitoring to identify
and report suspicious transactions. Beneficial ownership must be identified for
legal entity customers.

## Suspicious Activity Reporting (SAR)
Banks must file a SAR for transactions involving at least the applicable dollar
thresholds where the bank knows, suspects, or has reason to suspect suspicious
activity. Timeliness (generally within 30 days of detection) and quality of the
narrative are examination focus areas.

## Transaction Monitoring Systems
Automated transaction-monitoring and fraud systems used for AML should be
subject to model risk management: documented rules and thresholds, tuning and
above-the-line/below-the-line testing, data-quality validation, and ongoing
performance monitoring. Threshold changes require governance approval.

## Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)
Banks must file CTRs for cash transactions exceeding the reporting threshold and
guard against structuring designed to evade reporting.

## Model Tuning and Validation for AML
AML detection models should balance detection effectiveness against false
positives. Validation includes coverage assessment, threshold analysis,
sampling of alerts, and confirmation that productive alerts are not being
suppressed by mis-calibrated rules.

## Sanctions / OFAC Screening
Screening systems must be tuned to detect sanctioned parties while managing
false positives; fuzzy-matching configurations should be validated and
periodically reviewed.
