# FCRA — Fair Credit Reporting Act

## Permissible Purpose
A consumer reporting agency may furnish a consumer report only for permissible
purposes, such as a credit transaction, employment, or account review. Users of
consumer reports must certify the permissible purpose.

## Accuracy and Data Quality
Furnishers and users must follow reasonable procedures to assure maximum
possible accuracy of the information in consumer reports. Models consuming
bureau data (e.g. FICO, VantageScore) should validate data quality and vintage.

## Risk-Based Pricing and Adverse Action
If a consumer report is used and adverse action is taken, the user must provide
an adverse action notice identifying the consumer reporting agency and the key
factors adversely affecting the credit score, generally limited to four factors.

## Dispute Handling
Consumers have the right to dispute inaccurate information. Systems relying on
bureau data should support correction and re-scoring workflows.
