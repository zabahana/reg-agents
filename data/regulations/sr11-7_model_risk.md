# SR 11-7 — Supervisory Guidance on Model Risk Management

## Purpose and Scope
Model risk arises from the potential for adverse consequences from decisions
based on incorrect or misused model outputs. Banks should manage model risk
both from individual models and in the aggregate. The guidance applies to all
models used in business decision-making, including those developed in-house and
those purchased from vendors.

## Model Definition
A model is a quantitative method, system, or approach that applies statistical,
economic, financial, or mathematical theories, techniques, and assumptions to
process input data into quantitative estimates. Models include machine learning
and AI systems that produce scores, classifications, or forecasts.

## Effective Challenge
The core principle of model risk management is "effective challenge" —
critical analysis by objective, informed parties who can identify model
limitations and assumptions and produce appropriate changes. Effective
challenge depends on a combination of incentives, competence, and influence.

## Three Elements of a Sound Framework
Model risk management should include: (1) robust model development,
implementation, and use; (2) sound model validation; and (3) governance,
policies, and controls.

## Conceptual Soundness
Validation should assess the quality of model design and construction, review
documentation and empirical evidence supporting methods used, and confirm that
judgment exercised in design is well informed and consistent with published
research and sound industry practice.

## Ongoing Monitoring
Ongoing monitoring confirms that a model is appropriately implemented and is
being used and performing as intended. Monitoring should include process
verification and benchmarking, and should escalate performance degradation
(e.g. score drift, population stability shifts) for remediation.

## Outcomes Analysis and Backtesting
Validation should include outcomes analysis comparing model outputs to actual
outcomes. Backtesting is a form of outcomes analysis that compares forecasts to
observed values. Discrepancies should trigger investigation.

## Documentation
Model documentation should be sufficient for a knowledgeable third party to
understand the model's purpose, design, limitations, assumptions, data,
testing, and monitoring. Insufficient documentation is itself a model risk.

## Vendor and AI/ML Models
For vendor or third-party AI models, banks remain responsible for validation and
should obtain enough information to evaluate conceptual soundness, monitor
performance, and understand limitations even when the model is proprietary.
