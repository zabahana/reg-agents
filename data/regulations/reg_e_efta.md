# Regulation E — Electronic Fund Transfer Act (EFTA)

## Scope
Regulation E governs electronic fund transfers (EFTs) affecting consumer
accounts, including debit card transactions, ACH, ATM, and P2P transfers. It
establishes consumer rights and institution obligations for error resolution
and unauthorized transactions.

## Unauthorized Transactions and Liability Limits
Consumer liability for unauthorized EFTs is limited and depends on how promptly
the consumer reports the loss. Institutions must have procedures to distinguish
genuinely unauthorized transactions from authorized ones, which interacts with
fraud-model decisions and dispute handling.

## Error Resolution Timelines
Upon receiving notice of an alleged error, the institution must investigate and
resolve within prescribed timeframes (generally 10 business days, extendable to
45 days with provisional credit). Fraud-decisioning systems must support timely
dispute intake and re-scoring.

## Impact on Fraud Controls
Blocking or reversing transactions must be reconcilable with Regulation E
consumer-protection obligations. When a legitimate customer is declined,
institutions should provide a clear path to resolution and document the basis
for the decision.

## Recordkeeping
Institutions must retain evidence of compliance, including error investigations
and the data supporting automated decisions, sufficient to demonstrate
compliance to examiners.
