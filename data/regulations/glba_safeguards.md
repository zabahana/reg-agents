# Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) — Privacy and Safeguards

## Privacy Rule
Financial institutions must provide consumers with privacy notices describing
information-sharing practices and the right to opt out of certain sharing with
nonaffiliated third parties. Model development using customer data must respect
these limitations.

## Safeguards Rule
Institutions must maintain a comprehensive information security program with
administrative, technical, and physical safeguards appropriate to their size
and complexity. This includes access controls, encryption of customer
information in transit and at rest, and secure disposal.

## Data Governance for AI/ML
Training data containing nonpublic personal information (NPI) must be handled
under the security program: minimized, access-controlled, and, where feasible,
tokenized or de-identified. On-premise or private-cloud inference (e.g., NIM in
the bank's own environment) supports data-residency and confidentiality
requirements.

## Third-Party / Service Provider Oversight
Institutions must oversee service providers by contract and monitoring to
ensure they maintain appropriate safeguards for customer information, including
providers of AI models and inference services.

## Incident Response
The program must include a written incident-response plan addressing security
events affecting customer information, including notification obligations.
