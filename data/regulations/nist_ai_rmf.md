# NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

## Purpose
The NIST AI RMF is a voluntary framework to manage risks of AI systems and
promote trustworthy AI. It is increasingly referenced by financial regulators
as a benchmark for AI governance, complementing SR 11-7.

## Characteristics of Trustworthy AI
Trustworthy AI is: valid and reliable; safe; secure and resilient; accountable
and transparent; explainable and interpretable; privacy-enhanced; and fair with
harmful bias managed. These map directly to banking model-governance concerns.

## GOVERN Function
Establish a culture of risk management: policies, accountability structures,
roles, diversity of perspectives, and processes for oversight of AI across the
lifecycle, including third-party and generative AI.

## MAP Function
Establish context and identify risks: intended purpose, deployment setting,
stakeholders, and potential impacts. For an AI system, categorize its
capabilities, data, and the consequences of errors.

## MEASURE Function
Analyze, assess, and benchmark AI risks using quantitative and qualitative
methods: performance, robustness, bias/fairness, security (e.g., adversarial
robustness), and explainability. Track metrics over time and under drift.

## MANAGE Function
Prioritize and act on risks: allocate resources, implement controls, plan for
incident response and model retirement, and continuously monitor. Document
residual risk and decisions.

## Generative AI Considerations
For generative and agentic systems, additional risks include hallucination,
data leakage, prompt injection, and unsafe tool use. Evaluations, guardrails,
retrieval grounding, and human oversight are recommended mitigations.
