# ECOA / Regulation B — Equal Credit Opportunity

## Prohibited Basis
It is unlawful for a creditor to discriminate against any applicant with
respect to any aspect of a credit transaction on a prohibited basis: race,
color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided the
applicant can contract), receipt of public assistance, or exercise of rights
under the Consumer Credit Protection Act.

## Disparate Impact
A facially neutral policy or model that produces a disproportionately adverse
effect on a prohibited-basis group may violate ECOA unless it meets a
legitimate business need that cannot reasonably be achieved by a less
discriminatory alternative. Models should be tested for disparate impact.

## Adverse Action Notices
When a creditor takes adverse action (e.g. denial), it must provide a notice
with the specific principal reasons for the action. For model-based decisions,
the reasons must accurately reflect the factors actually used by the model.
"Black box" scores that cannot produce accurate reasons create compliance risk.

## Model Feature Governance
Creditors should ensure that model features do not act as proxies for protected
classes (e.g. ZIP code as a proxy for race). Feature selection and monitoring
should include fair-lending review.
