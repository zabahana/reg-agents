# CCAR / DFAST — Capital Planning and Stress Testing

## Overview
The Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review (CCAR) and Dodd-Frank Act Stress
Testing (DFAST) require large banking organizations to demonstrate capital
adequacy under baseline, adverse, and severely adverse macroeconomic scenarios.

## Supervisory Scenarios
The Federal Reserve publishes scenarios defined by macroeconomic variables
(GDP, unemployment, rates, house prices, market shocks). Models translate these
into projected losses, revenues (PPNR), and capital ratios.

## Model Landscape
Stress testing relies on many interconnected models: PPNR (pre-provision net
revenue), credit loss (PD/LGD/EAD), balance projection, and market/counterparty
risk. Each is subject to SR 11-7 model risk management.

## Model Risk in Stress Testing
Key risks include scenario sensitivity, segment-level bias, thin data in
downturn segments, and static assumptions (e.g., recovery timing). Validation
should include benchmarking, backtesting where feasible, and sensitivity
analysis across scenarios.

## Conservatism and Overlays
Where data is limited or uncertainty is high, conservative assumptions and
documented management overlays are expected, with rationale and governance
sign-off.

## Documentation and Governance
Firms must maintain robust documentation of methodologies, assumptions,
limitations, and validation, and demonstrate strong board and senior-management
governance over the capital-planning process.
