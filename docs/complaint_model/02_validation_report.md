## 2 · Assessment narrative

The two-stage complaint classification model, comprising stage-1 TF-IDF classifiers and stage-2 retrieval-augmented generation pipeline (rag_llm), has been thoroughly examined in this validation report. The model's conceptual soundness, data quality, outcomes analysis, monitoring and guardrails adequacy, and consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure of complaint routing have been scrutinized.

Conceptual soundness is a critical aspect of any model, and the two-stage design employed here is a common approach in complaint classification tasks. However, it is essential to maintain the assumptions underlying this design. The stage-1 classifiers are expected to provide a robust feature representation, which is then refined by the stage-2 rag_llm pipeline. The assumption that the stage-1 classifiers are effective in capturing the relevant features is critical to the overall performance of the model. If this assumption is not met, the stage-2 pipeline may not be able to improve upon the stage-1 performance.

The data quality is a significant concern, given the weak-supervision reference used to derive the labels. The CFPB product/issue taxonomy and narrative keyword rules are used to assign labels to the complaint narratives. While this approach is common in the absence of human-adjudicated labels, it introduces a level of uncertainty and potential bias in the labels. This, in turn, affects every downstream metric, including the distinction between measured agreement and true accuracy. The validation report flags this as a limitation, and it is essential to acknowledge the potential impact on the model's performance.

The outcomes analysis reveals a mixed picture. The stage-1 TF-IDF classifiers perform well, with the logistic_regression model achieving a pr_auc of 0.9962 and an accuracy of 0.95. However, the stage-2 rag_llm pipeline struggles, with an accuracy of 0.3652 and a family_accuracy of 0.5478. The recall values for individual labels are also concerning, with several labels having a recall of 0.0. This suggests that the stage-2 pipeline may not be effective in capturing the nuances of the complaint narratives.

The monitoring and guardrails adequacy are also critical aspects of the model's performance. The model's ability to detect and respond to changes in the complaint landscape is essential to its effectiveness. However, the validation report does not provide sufficient information to assess the adequacy of the monitoring and guardrails.

The consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure of complaint routing is a significant concern. The model's ability to route complaints to the relevant regulatory agencies is critical to ensuring that consumers receive the necessary protection. However, the validation report does not provide sufficient information to assess the model's performance in this regard.

In light of these findings, the following validation findings are presented:

V-1: **High** - The model's conceptual soundness is compromised by the assumption that the stage-1 classifiers are effective in capturing the relevant features. Remediation: Re-evaluate the stage-1 classifiers and refine the feature representation to improve the stage-2 pipeline's performance. Owner: Model Development Team.

V-2: **High** - The data quality is compromised by the weak-supervision reference used to derive the labels. Remediation: Consider using human-adjudicated labels or alternative labeling approaches to improve the label quality. Owner: Data Science Team.

V-3: **Medium** - The stage-2 rag_llm pipeline struggles to achieve high accuracy and recall values. Remediation: Refine the rag_llm pipeline and explore alternative approaches to improve its performance. Owner: Model Development Team.

V-4: **Medium** - The monitoring and guardrails adequacy are not sufficient to assess the model's performance. Remediation: Develop and implement a robust monitoring and guardrails framework to ensure the model's effectiveness. Owner: Model Operations Team.

V-5: **High** - The consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure of complaint routing is not adequately addressed. Remediation: Develop and implement a robust complaint routing framework to ensure that consumers receive the necessary protection. Owner: Model Operations Team.

In light of these findings, the disposition of the validation report is:

Approve with Conditions

Conditions:

1. The model development team must re-evaluate the stage-1 classifiers and refine the feature representation to improve the stage-2 pipeline's performance.
2. The data science team must consider using human-adjudicated labels or alternative labeling approaches to improve the label quality.
3. The model development team must refine the rag_llm pipeline and explore alternative approaches to improve its performance.
4. The model operations team must develop and implement a robust monitoring and guardrails framework to ensure the model's effectiveness.
5. The model operations team must develop and implement a robust complaint routing framework to ensure that consumers receive the necessary protection.

These conditions must be met before the model can be deployed in production.

## 4 · Key limitations (validator-confirmed)

1. **Weak labels** — ground truth derives from the CFPB issue taxonomy +
   keyword rules, not human adjudication; stage-2 "accuracy" is agreement with
   a noisy reference. Condition: human-adjudicated golden set (≥25/category).
2. **Class imbalance** — several categories (SCRA/MLA, sales practices,
   adverse action) have thin support; per-category metrics are unstable there.
3. **LLM nondeterminism & drift** — prompt/model versions must be pinned and
   re-evaluated on the golden set before any provider/model change (the system
   records provider + model in every artifact banner).
4. **Fair-lending lens** — complaint routing affects remediation speed;
   monitoring should include parity of routing outcomes across products.

## 5 · Disposition

**Approve with Conditions** — see conditions in the assessment narrative and
limitations above; re-validation triggered by model/prompt change or stage-1
PSI > 0.25.
