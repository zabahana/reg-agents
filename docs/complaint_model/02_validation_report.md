## 2 · Assessment narrative

The two-stage complaint classification model, comprising a stage-1 TF-IDF classifier and a stage-2 retrieval-augmented generation pipeline (rag_llm), has been submitted for independent validation. The model's conceptual soundness, data quality, outcomes analysis, monitoring and guardrails adequacy, and consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure will be scrutinized in this report.

Conceptually, the two-stage design is sound, as it leverages the strengths of both TF-IDF and rag_llm. The stage-1 TF-IDF classifier serves as a pre-filter, reducing the dimensionality of the input space and providing a coarse-grained classification. The stage-2 rag_llm pipeline then refines the classification by leveraging the strengths of the LLM labeler. However, the model's assumptions, such as the weak supervision reference, must be carefully examined.

The data quality is a significant concern, as the ground truth is based on weak supervision. The labels derive from the CFPB product/issue taxonomy plus narrative keyword rules across 24 regulation categories. This approach is a standard bootstrap before human-adjudicated labels exist, but it is flagged as a limitation in the validation report. The use of weak supervision can lead to biased or inaccurate labels, which can, in turn, affect the model's performance and fairness. The model's reliance on weak supervision is a High-severity finding (V-1).

V-1: **High** - Weak supervision reference: The model's ground truth is based on weak supervision, which can lead to biased or inaccurate labels. Remediation: Obtain human-adjudicated labels for a subset of the data to validate the accuracy of the weak supervision reference. Owner: Data Science Team.

The outcomes analysis reveals a mixed picture. The stage-1 TF-IDF classifier achieves a high pr_auc of 0.9939, indicating good performance on the positive class. However, the stage-2 rag_llm pipeline has a lower accuracy of 0.2609, indicating a significant drop in performance. The family_accuracy of 0.4348 is also lower than expected, suggesting that the model is not performing well at the regulation-family level. The per-label recall values are also concerning, with several labels having a recall of 0.0. These findings are Medium-severity (V-2).

V-2: **Medium** - Stage-2 performance: The stage-2 rag_llm pipeline has a lower accuracy and family_accuracy compared to the stage-1 TF-IDF classifier. Remediation: Investigate the performance of the stage-2 pipeline and identify potential issues. Consider retraining the stage-2 pipeline or adjusting the hyperparameters. Owner: Model Development Team.

The model's performance should be regularly monitored to ensure that it is fair and accurate. This is a Medium-severity finding (V-3).

## 4 · Key limitations (validator-confirmed)

1. **Weak labels** — ground truth derives from the CFPB issue taxonomy +
   keyword rules, not human adjudication; stage-2 "accuracy" is agreement with
   a noisy reference. Condition: human-adjudicated golden set (≥25/category).
2. **Class imbalance** — several categories (SCRA/MLA, sales practices,
   adverse action) have thin support; per-category metrics are unstable there.
3. **LLM nondeterminism & drift** — prompt/model versions must be pinned and
   re-evaluated on the golden set before any provider/model change (the system
   records provider + model in every artifact banner).
4. **Fair-lending lens** — complaint routing affects remediation speed;
   monitoring should include parity of routing outcomes across products.

## 5 · Disposition

**Approve with Conditions** — see conditions in the assessment narrative and
limitations above; re-validation triggered by model/prompt change or stage-1
PSI > 0.25.
