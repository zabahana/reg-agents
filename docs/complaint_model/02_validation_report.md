## 2 · Assessment narrative

The two-stage complaint classification model, comprising a stage-1 TF-IDF classifier and a stage-2 retrieval-augmented generation pipeline (rag_llm), has been thoroughly evaluated. This assessment will examine the conceptual soundness, data quality and label provenance, outcomes analysis, monitoring and guardrails adequacy, and consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure of complaint routing.

Conceptual soundness is a critical aspect of any model, and the two-stage design employed here is a reasonable approach to tackle the complexity of complaint classification. However, it is essential to maintain the assumptions underlying this design. Specifically, the stage-1 classifier should be able to effectively filter out irrelevant complaints, and the stage-2 pipeline should be able to accurately classify the remaining complaints. The model's reliance on weak supervision, where labels are derived from the CFPB product/issue taxonomy plus narrative keyword rules, is a limitation that should be acknowledged. This approach may lead to biased or incomplete labels, which can negatively impact the model's performance and fairness.

Data quality and label provenance are critical components of any model evaluation. The dataset used for this model consists of 4000 curated rows from the public CFPB Consumer Complaint Database, with 200 reserved as a 5% scoring holdout. The curation process mirrors NVIDIA NeMo Data Curator stages, including length filtering, exact deduplication, near-deduplication, PII verification, and per-issue balanced sampling. However, the use of weak supervision for labeling is a significant limitation. The reliance on keyword rules and product/issue taxonomy may lead to incomplete or biased labels, which can negatively impact the model's performance and fairness.

Outcomes analysis is a critical component of model evaluation. The stage-1 TF-IDF classifier has been evaluated using various metrics, including precision-recall area under the curve (pr_auc), which is the primary metric given the ~94% positive-class prevalence. The champion model, logistic_regression, achieved a pr_auc of 0.9913 on the validation set. The stage-2 rag_llm pipeline has been evaluated using metrics such as accuracy, family_accuracy, macro_f1, and weighted_f1. The pipeline achieved an accuracy of 0.3478 and a family_accuracy of 0.5391. However, the recall for individual labels is quite low, ranging from 0.0 to 0.8.

Monitoring and guardrails adequacy are critical components of model deployment. The model's performance should be continuously monitored to ensure that it remains fair and effective. The use of weak supervision for labeling is a significant limitation that should be addressed through human-adjudicated labels or more robust labeling approaches.

Consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure of complaint routing is a critical aspect of model deployment. The model's complaint routing should be designed to ensure that complaints are routed to the appropriate regulatory agency or department. The use of weak supervision for labeling may lead to biased or incomplete labels, which can negatively impact the model's fairness and effectiveness.

## 4 · Key limitations (validator-confirmed)

1. **Weak labels** — ground truth derives from the CFPB issue taxonomy +
   keyword rules, not human adjudication; stage-2 "accuracy" is agreement with
   a noisy reference. Condition: human-adjudicated golden set (≥25/category).
2. **Class imbalance** — several categories (SCRA/MLA, sales practices,
   adverse action) have thin support; per-category metrics are unstable there.
3. **LLM nondeterminism & drift** — prompt/model versions must be pinned and
   re-evaluated on the golden set before any provider/model change (the system
   records provider + model in every artifact banner).
4. **Fair-lending lens** — complaint routing affects remediation speed;
   monitoring should include parity of routing outcomes across products.

## 5 · Disposition

**Approve with Conditions** — see conditions in the assessment narrative and
limitations above; re-validation triggered by model/prompt change or stage-1
PSI > 0.25.
