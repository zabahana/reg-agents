## 2 · Assessment narrative

The two-stage complaint classification model, comprising a stage-1 TF-IDF classifier and a stage-2 retrieval-augmented generation pipeline (rag_llm), has been evaluated for conceptual soundness, data quality, outcomes analysis, monitoring and guardrails adequacy, and consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure. The findings are presented below.

Conceptual soundness is a key consideration for the two-stage design. The model's architecture, which combines a stage-1 TF-IDF classifier with a stage-2 rag_llm, is conceptually sound. However, the model's performance is heavily dependent on the quality of the weak-supervision reference, which is derived from the CFPB product/issue taxonomy plus narrative keyword rules across 24 regulation categories. This raises concerns about the model's ability to generalize to unseen data and its potential for bias. The model's performance could fail if the weak-supervision reference is not representative of the underlying data distribution.

Data quality and label provenance are critical aspects of the model's performance. The data source is real, redacted consumer complaint narratives from the public CFPB Consumer Complaint Database. However, the data curation process, which includes length filtering, exact deduplication, near-deduplication, PII verification, and per-issue balanced sampling, may not be sufficient to ensure data quality. The use of weak supervision, which derives labels from the CFPB product/issue taxonomy plus narrative keyword rules, is a limitation of the model. This approach may lead to biased labels and affect the model's performance. The distinction between measured agreement and true accuracy is also blurred due to the weak-supervision reference.

Outcomes analysis reveals that the stage-1 TF-IDF classifier performs well, with a pr_auc of 0.9957 and a f1 of 0.9569. However, the stage-2 rag_llm performs poorly, with an accuracy of 0.3913 and a family_accuracy of 0.5652. The macro_f1 and weighted_f1 of the stage-2 rag_llm are also low, indicating poor performance. The recall values for individual labels are also low, ranging from 0.0 to 1.0. The small-support instability of the stage-2 rag_llm is a concern, as it may lead to biased results. The family-level agreement diverges from exact agreement, indicating that the model's performance is not consistent across different regulation categories.

Monitoring and guardrails adequacy are critical aspects of the model's performance. The model's performance is monitored using metrics such as pr_auc, f1, and accuracy. However, the model's performance is not adequately guarded against bias and overfitting. The use of weak supervision and the stage-2 rag_llm's poor performance raise concerns about the model's ability to generalize to unseen data.

Consumer-protection/fair-lending exposure is a critical consideration for the model's performance. The model's performance may be biased against certain regulation categories, such as FCRA and FDCPA, which have a lower recall value. The model's performance may also be affected by the weak-supervision reference, which may lead to biased labels.

Based on the findings, the following validation report is issued:

**Validation Report**

**Conceptual Soundness**

* V-1: The two-stage design is conceptually sound, but the model's performance is heavily dependent on the quality of the weak-supervision reference. Severity: Medium. Remediation: Improve data curation and label provenance. Owner: Data Science Team.
* V-2: The model's performance could fail if the weak-supervision reference is not representative of the underlying data distribution. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve data curation and label provenance. Owner: Data Science Team.

**Data Quality and Label Provenance**

* V-3: The data curation process may not be sufficient to ensure data quality. Severity: Medium. Remediation: Improve data curation process. Owner: Data Science Team.
* V-4: The use of weak supervision is a limitation of the model. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve label provenance. Owner: Data Science Team.

**Outcomes Analysis**

* V-5: The stage-1 TF-IDF classifier performs well, but the stage-2 rag_llm performs poorly. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve stage-2 rag_llm performance. Owner: Model Development Team.
* V-6: The small-support instability of the stage-2 rag_llm is a concern. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve stage-2 rag_llm performance. Owner: Model Development Team.
* V-7: The family-level agreement diverges from exact agreement. Severity: Medium. Remediation: Improve model performance. Owner: Model Development Team.

**Monitoring and Guardrails Adequacy**

* V-8: The model's performance is not adequately guarded against bias and overfitting. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve monitoring and guardrails. Owner: Model Development Team.

**Consumer-Protection/Fair-Lending Exposure**

* V-9: The model's performance may be biased against certain regulation categories. Severity: High. Remediation: Improve model performance. Owner: Model Development Team.

**Disposition**

The model is approved with conditions. The conditions are as follows:

1. Improve data curation and label provenance to ensure data quality and reduce bias.
2. Improve stage-2 rag_llm performance to reduce small-support instability and improve family-level agreement.
3. Improve monitoring and guardrails to ensure the model's performance is not biased against certain regulation categories.
4. Improve model performance to reduce bias and improve family-level agreement.

The model development team is responsible for implementing the remediations and owners are responsible for ensuring the remediations are implemented.

## 4 · Key limitations (validator-confirmed)

1. **Weak labels** — ground truth derives from the CFPB issue taxonomy +
   keyword rules, not human adjudication; stage-2 "accuracy" is agreement with
   a noisy reference. Condition: human-adjudicated golden set (≥25/category).
2. **Class imbalance** — several categories (SCRA/MLA, sales practices,
   adverse action) have thin support; per-category metrics are unstable there.
3. **LLM nondeterminism & drift** — prompt/model versions must be pinned and
   re-evaluated on the golden set before any provider/model change (the system
   records provider + model in every artifact banner).
4. **Fair-lending lens** — complaint routing affects remediation speed;
   monitoring should include parity of routing outcomes across products.

## 5 · Disposition

**Approve with Conditions** — see conditions in the assessment narrative and
limitations above; re-validation triggered by model/prompt change or stage-1
PSI > 0.25.
