# Audit Report on Model Risk Management Process

## 1. Objective & Scope
The objective of this audit is to assess the effectiveness of the model risk management process for the fraud detection model developed to predict fraudulent card transactions. The scope includes reviewing the model development documentation, the independent validation report, and governance regulations to evaluate the adequacy of controls in place throughout the model's lifecycle, from development to independent validation and approval.

## 2. Process Walkthrough
The model risk management process was reviewed in three key stages:

### Development
- The model was developed to predict fraudulent transactions using a dataset of 1,000 card transaction records. The Decision Tree model was selected based on its highest ROC AUC score.

### Independent Validation
- The independent validation report assessed the model's conceptual soundness, effective challenge of model selection, and data outcomes. Concerns were raised regarding the model's low recall and class imbalance.

### Approval
- The model was approved with conditions, contingent upon addressing identified issues, particularly regarding recall and class imbalance, and conducting ongoing monitoring and fair-lending assessments.

## 3. Control Assessment
The following controls were assessed for effectiveness:

- **Independence of Validation from Development**: The validation team operated independently from the development team, ensuring an unbiased review of the model.
  
- **Documentation Completeness**: The model development document and validation report were comprehensive, covering purpose, data, candidate models, performance metrics, assumptions, limitations, and proposed monitoring strategies.

- **Evidence of Effective Challenge**: The validation report highlighted the need for a more balanced model selection criterion, indicating an effective challenge to the development team's decisions.

- **Issue Tracking**: The validation report identified key issues, including low recall and class imbalance, with recommendations for remediation.

- **Approval Before Deployment**: The model was conditionally approved, ensuring that identified issues must be addressed prior to deployment.

## 4. Audit Findings & Issues
The following findings were identified, rated by severity:

1. **Low Recall of Champion Model** (High)
   - Owner: Model Development Team
   - Recommendation: Re-evaluate model selection criteria to prioritize recall.

2. **Class Imbalance Not Adequately Addressed** (High)
   - Owner: Model Development Team
   - Recommendation: Implement techniques to address class imbalance.

3. **Small Dataset Size** (Medium)
   - Owner: Model Development Team
   - Recommendation: Expand the dataset or apply cross-validation techniques.

4. **Lack of Fair-Lending Review** (Medium)
   - Owner: Compliance Team
   - Recommendation: Conduct a comprehensive fair-lending analysis during validation and ongoing monitoring.

## 5. Audit Opinion
**Audit Opinion: Needs Improvement**

### Justification
The model risk management process demonstrates a structured approach to model development and validation; however, significant issues were identified that could impact the model's effectiveness in detecting fraudulent transactions. The low recall and inadequate handling of class imbalance pose substantial risks, necessitating immediate remediation. Additionally, the lack of a thorough fair-lending review raises compliance concerns. Addressing these issues is critical to align with governance requirements and ensure the model's reliability and fairness in decision-making processes.
