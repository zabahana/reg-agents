**RECOMMENDATION:** Conduct a comprehensive review of the GNN typologies to identify and incorporate additional features that enhance coverage for trade-based money laundering. #### 2. Data Quality
**GAP:** The documentation lacks clarity on the validation of the sanctions fuzzy-match configuration, which is critical for ensuring that the model effectively identifies transactions involving sanctioned entities.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Implement a thorough validation process for the sanctions fuzzy-match configuration. Document the methodology and results of this validation to ensure compliance with data quality standards outlined in the FFIEC guidelines [ffiec_bsa_aml.md].

#### 3. Testing/Outcomes Analysis
**GAP:** The documentation mentions periodic ATL/BTL testing but does not specify the cadence or methodology for this testing, which is essential for understanding the model's performance over time.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Establish a clear and documented cadence for ATL/BTL testing, including the methodology used for sampling and analysis. This should align with the model risk management practices required for AML systems [ffiec_bsa_aml.md].

#### 4. Ongoing Monitoring
**GAP:** While the model includes monthly tracking of productive and false-positive rates, there is no mention of how the results of this monitoring are used to inform model adjustments or improvements.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Develop a structured process for utilizing the results of ongoing monitoring to inform model recalibrations and enhancements. This should include a feedback loop that integrates monitoring outcomes into the model governance framework [ffiec_bsa_aml.md].

#### 5. Fair-Lending/ECOA Exposure
**GAP:** The model documentation states that there are no protected class features included in the model. However, the potential for indirect discrimination through the model's outputs should be assessed, especially considering the high-risk nature of the transactions being monitored.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Conduct a fair-lending impact assessment to evaluate any indirect biases that may arise from the model's outputs. Ensure that the model complies with ECOA requirements by assessing whether the model's decisions could disproportionately affect certain groups, even if no protected class features are directly included [udaap_bsa_aml.md].

## Appendix C — Retriever Agent regulatory context

The regulatory requirements for validating and monitoring a model used for detecting suspicious activity for SAR triage (BSA/AML) include the following points:

1. **Validation and Explainability**: Transaction-monitoring and fraud models used for AML must be validated, explainable, and tuned to balance detection against false positives. This ensures that the models are effective and transparent in their operations. [udaap_bsa_aml.md, BSA/AML — Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering]

2. **Model Risk Management**: Automated transaction-monitoring and fraud systems used for AML should be subject to model risk management practices. This includes having documented rules and thresholds, tuning, above-the-line/below-the-line testing, data-quality validation, and ongoing performance monitoring. Any changes to thresholds require governance approval. [ffiec_bsa_aml.md, Transaction Monitoring Systems]

3. **Governance and Reporting**: There should be integration of fair-lending risk into model risk management, which includes fair-lending review at development, validation, and ongoing monitoring, with results reported to governance committees. [fair_lending_exam.md, Governance Expectations]

4. **Principles-Based Guidance**: The guidance for model risk management is principles-based and scales with the bank's size, complexity, and model use. This means that the specific requirements may vary based on the institution's characteristics. [occ_2011-12_model_risk.md, Relationship to SR 11-7]

These points outline the essential regulatory requirements for validating and monitoring models used in detecting suspicious activities related to BSA/AML compliance.
