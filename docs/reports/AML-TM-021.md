## Appendix C — Retriever Agent regulatory context

The regulatory requirements for validating and monitoring a model used for detecting suspicious activity for SAR triage (BSA/AML) include the following:

1. **Validation and Explainability**: Transaction-monitoring and fraud models used for AML must be validated, explainable, and tuned to balance detection against false positives. This ensures that the models are effective in identifying suspicious activities without generating excessive false alarms. [Source: udaap_bsa_aml.md, BSA/AML — Bank Secrecy Act / Anti-Money Laundering]

2. **Model Risk Management**: Automated transaction-monitoring and fraud systems used for AML should be subject to model risk management practices. This includes having documented rules and thresholds, tuning, above-the-line/below-the-line testing, data-quality validation, and ongoing performance monitoring. Any changes to thresholds require governance approval, ensuring that there is oversight and accountability in the model's operation. [Source: ffiec_bsa_aml.md, Transaction Monitoring Systems]

3. **Governance and Reporting**: There should be integration of fair-lending risk into model risk management, which includes fair-lending reviews at development, validation, and ongoing monitoring stages, with results reported to governance committees. This emphasizes the need for oversight and structured governance in the model management process. [Source: fair_lending_exam.md, Governance Expectations]

4. **Principles-Based Guidance**: The guidance on model risk management is principles-based and scales with the bank's size, complexity, and model use. This means that the requirements can be adapted based on the specific context of the bank and the models it employs. [Source: occ_2011-12_model_risk.md, Relationship to SR 11-7]

These points collectively highlight the importance of rigorous validation, ongoing monitoring, governance, and adherence to principles-based guidance in managing models used for detecting suspicious activities in the context of BSA/AML compliance.
